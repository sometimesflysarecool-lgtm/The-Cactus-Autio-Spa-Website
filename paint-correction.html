Good To Know

What a One-Step Polish Does Not Do

A one-step polish is a paint enhancement service, not a full paint correction. It will NOT remove deep scratches, heavy swirl marks, water spot etching, or other significant paint defects. For those issues, a multi-step correction would be required. We believe in being upfront so you know exactly what results to expect.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between paint enhancement and full paint correction?
Paint enhancement is a one-step polish — it removes light oxidation, restores gloss, and rejuvenates dull or faded paint. Full paint correction involves multiple heavier cutting stages to remove deep scratches, heavy swirl marks, and water spot etching. If your paint just looks flat or hazy, enhancement will make a noticeable difference at a fraction of the cost. If there are significant visible defects, correction is what you need.
Will paint enhancement remove scratches and swirl marks?
It can reduce very light surface scratches and minor swirling, but it won't eliminate deep scratches or heavy swirl marks. We're upfront about this: a one-step polish is not a substitute for multi-step paint correction.
How long do the results last?
Results typically last 3–6 months depending on how the vehicle is maintained, where it's parked, and Arizona's intense sun exposure. Pairing paint enhancement with a ceramic coating can significantly extend how long the results hold.
Is paint enhancement worth doing before a ceramic coating?
Yes — it's actually ideal preparation. Starting with clean, polished paint means the ceramic coating bonds properly and looks its best. Applying a coating over dull or oxidized paint locks those imperfections in permanently.
How long does the service take?
Most paint enhancement services take 2–4 hours depending on the size of your vehicle. We're fully mobile — we come to your home or workplace in Mesa or Gilbert, AZ, so there's no drop-off or waiting at a shop.
